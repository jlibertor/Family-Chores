# Family Chores — Future Phases 13 Through 15

## Purpose

Phases 1–12 established:

- stable household workflows
- mounted kiosk experiences
- lightweight reminders
- long-term maintainability
- simplicity preservation

These final exploratory phases focus on:

- preserving usefulness over years
- maintaining household engagement
- reducing maintenance burden
- keeping the app calm and dependable

The app should still remain:

```text
small
practical
understandable
```

---

# Phase 13 — Long-Term Household Habits and Routine Support

## Goal

Help reinforce healthy household routines without turning the app into a productivity system.

The purpose is:

```text
gentle household consistency
```

not surveillance or pressure.

---

## Suggested Features

### Household Rhythm Indicators

Examples:

```text
Most chores completed today
Kitchen reset complete
Weekly cleanup mostly done
```

Keep messaging positive and lightweight.

Avoid guilt-heavy systems.

---

## Optional Household Goals

Examples:

```text
Complete all daily chores before 8 PM
Keep kitchen streak for one week
```

Goals should remain:

- optional
- simple
- easy to disable

Avoid building achievement frameworks.

---

## Family-Friendly Encouragement

Examples:

```text
Great teamwork today
All chores complete
Nice job keeping up this week
```

Keep tone calm and encouraging.

Avoid excessive gamification.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

Phase 13 is complete when:

- routines feel slightly easier to maintain
- the app encourages consistency gently
- household friction does not increase
- the app still feels lightweight

---

# Phase 14 — Simplified Household Hub Features

## Goal

Allow the app to become a small shared household hub without losing focus.

The app should still feel:

```text
minimal
focused
fast
```

---

## Suggested Features

### Shared Quick Notes

Examples:

```text
Need milk
Trash pickup tomorrow
Dog medicine tonight
```

Requirements:

- very fast entry
- simple display
- no rich editing

Avoid building collaborative note software.

---

## Shared Household Announcements

Examples:

```text
Movie night Friday
Grandma visiting Saturday
Pool maintenance tomorrow
```

Keep temporary and lightweight.

---

## Optional Quick Buttons

Examples:

```text
Mark kitchen reset complete
Mark trash to curb
Mark pets fed
```

These should reduce repetitive taps.

Only implement if genuinely useful.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

Phase 14 is complete when:

- the app becomes slightly more useful as a household hub
- the interface remains uncluttered
- household coordination improves slightly
- app speed and simplicity remain intact

---

# Phase 15 — Preservation, Longevity, and Minimal Maintenance

## Goal

Prepare the project to remain usable for years with minimal maintenance.

This phase prioritizes:

- durability
- simplicity
- maintainability
- cleanup

rather than feature growth.

---

## Suggested Work

### Dependency Cleanup

Review:

- unnecessary packages
- outdated libraries
- oversized dependencies
- abandoned tooling

Keep the project lean.

---

## UI Simplification Pass

Review:

- confusing screens
- excessive settings
- rarely used flows
- redundant actions

Prefer removing friction over adding features.

---

## Long-Term Stability Review

Review:

- tablet performance
- browser compatibility
- Cloudflare deployment simplicity
- D1 schema cleanliness
- local persistence reliability

The app should remain stable on older devices.

---

## Backup and Recovery Review

Ensure:

- lightweight export exists
- household setup can be restored easily
- documentation explains deployment and recovery

Avoid enterprise backup systems.

---

## Documentation Preservation

Ensure:

- docs remain accurate
- handoff folders remain organized
- completed work archived
- architecture easy for future AI agents to understand

The repository should remain readable years later.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

Phase 15 is complete when:

- app requires minimal maintenance
- household usage remains smooth
- project remains understandable
- mounted tablet workflow remains reliable
- future development remains easy

---

# Explicit Non-Goals

Still intentionally excluded:

- enterprise platforms
- workforce management
- corporate dashboards
- billing systems
- complex AI orchestration
- social media systems
- advanced automation engines
- complicated customization frameworks
